About Neuschwanstein Castle

Neuschwanstein Castle is a 19th-century Romanesque Revival palace located in Bavaria, Germany. It was commissioned by King Ludwig II of Bavaria and served as his private retreat and homage to Richard Wagner, the renowned composer.

The castle's fairy-tale-like appearance, nestled in the Bavarian Alps, has inspired numerous artists and filmmakers, making it one of the most famous castles in the world. Its picturesque setting and architectural grandeur attract millions of visitors annually.
