About Brandenburg Gate

The Brandenburg Gate is an 18th-century neoclassical monument in Berlin, built on the orders of Prussian king Frederick William II. It has become one of the most iconic symbols of Berlin and Germany.

The gate stands at the western end of the Unter den Linden boulevard in the heart of Berlin. It represents peace and unity and has witnessed significant historical events, including the division and reunification of Germany.

Visitors can admire the gate's architecture, learn about its history in the nearby museums, and enjoy the bustling atmosphere of Pariser Platz, the square adjacent to the gate.
