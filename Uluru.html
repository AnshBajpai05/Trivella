About Uluru

Uluru, also known as Ayers Rock, is a large sandstone rock formation located in the Northern Territory of Australia. It is a sacred site for the indigenous Anangu people and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Uluru is one of Australia's most iconic landmarks, known for its stunning natural beauty and spiritual significance. The rock formation stands 348 meters high and measures 9.4 kilometers in circumference, making it an impressive sight to behold.

Visitors to Uluru can explore the surrounding Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park, participate in guided tours to learn about the rock's cultural significance, and enjoy breathtaking sunsets and starry skies. The area is also rich in wildlife and offers opportunities for hiking and photography.
